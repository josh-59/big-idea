Formal

Abstractions   An abstraction is a simplification; abstracting discards information in order to produce something simpler. The existence of an abstraction implies the existence of an abstracting process.

The Process   This paper introduces the process, a function without fixed points. All processes make change; a process is a set of changes.

Economy   This paper introduces the notion of the economy of a process, how efficiently it carries out its change. Economy will form our formal foundation for intelligence.

Intelligence   Building upon economy and order, we can begin to make formal statements about intelligence.

Reasonableness   This paper begins to build, formally, on processes.

Intellect   My target object. This will be an "abstracting engine".

Anecdotal

Bridges   Languages are funny things: They exist in their own right, and yet they exist to facilitate something else. When do you have enough of a bridge?

A More Formal View of Thinking   Here, we take another look at thinking. The idea that thinking is a process is introduced.

Abstraction in Action   In this essay, an example of thinking by way of abstraction is presented.

Processes Tracing Objects   Some demonstrations of the way that a process can be used to "create" an object are presented.

Looking Forward   This development takes time, and I thought I would post the parts of the theory which I look forward to.

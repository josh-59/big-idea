Tracing Abstractions

Consider the following depiction of a second-order abstracting process:

Each realized process traces a finite sequence of abstractions. The first realized process terminates at $(1)$, then it jumps to $(2)$, tracing another sequence.

The salient point that this expresses is that $(2)$ is later in time than $(1)$. More, at $(2)$, the process is re-viewing the abstractions traced by the realized process terminating at $(1)$.

This is an avenue for change; if the process changes implicitly through the course of a trace, then re-veiwing is nontrivial.

About

"For an abstraction to be worthy of study,       
      it must have many diverse concrete realizations."

My name is Josh Timmons, and I'm a mathematics student with a keen interest in computers. I started college a little late, taking a detour to serve in the U.S. Army. At 25 years old, I went off to college, originally pursuing mechanical engineering. Physics is not my strong suit, but math stayed with me; I wound up changing my major in my sophomore year. Within math, I like abstract algebra and mathematical logic.

As a mathematics major, I sought a minor in computer science to round out my education, earn credits and satisfy some curiosity that remained from childhood. After making a serious study of it in college, though, it became passion. I like understanding how the machine works at the deepest levels relevant to the programmer, and I especially like writing about it. You should check out my other project, Need to Know!

In college, an honest effort to learn and diligence regarding study has accrued into something I can be proud of: I currently have a GPA of 3.82, and will be graduating in the Spring.

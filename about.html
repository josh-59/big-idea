About

"For an abstraction to be worthy of study,       
      it must have many diverse concrete realizations."
Joseph A. Gallian

My name is Josh Timmons, and I'm a mathematics student with an interest in computers. I started college a little late, taking a detour to serve in the U.S. Army. At 25 years old, I went off to college, originally pursuing mechanical engineering. Physics is not my strong suit, but math stayed with me; I wound up changing my major in my sophomore year. Within math, I like abstract algebra and mathematical logic.

As a mathematics major, I sought a minor in computer science to round out my education, earn credits and satisfy some curiosity that remained from childhood. After making a serious study of it in college, though, it became a passion. I like understanding how the machine works at the deepest levels relevant to the programmer, and I especially like writing about it. You should check out my other project, Learning Linux!

Finally, I wish to say that computer science provided the genesis of this entire development— specifically, Joel Spolsky's The Law of Leaky Abstractions. To express my appreciation, and for a clear example of an abstraction, I ask that you read his essay.

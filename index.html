Welcome

Hello,

This website presents an attempt to describe, in gory detail, the operation of the mind.

I believe that the mind is principally defined by its memory, and the properties of that memory.

Except where otherwise noted, all ideas presented here are my own. I've been working on these ideas for some time, and would like to put them into the public domain. Although I've done my best, I wish to consider each essay to be up for review; the purpose of the website is to motivate me to complete the exposition of these ideas, and to improve them through the criticism of others.

Enjoy!

       – Josh

Changelog

Tuesday, January 12: Settled on a name for the observation-evaluation-resolution sequence: Thread. Made the observation that thought is the vehicle by which the mind simplifies itself; included it in Thought. May end up discarding abstraction as a fundamental operation; see Thought for more detail.

Monday, January 11: Thought: Made more precise the model being considered. Introduced an explanation of the diagram that was added yesterday. Removed references to precise and vague thinking: They aren't of central interest to this discussion.

While thought is the most interesting operation of the mind, it is not the "full workings". Rather, it is potentially a relatively small aspect of the operation of the mind.

Saturday, January 9: Continuing to work on Thought. Reduced the content and restricted the definition to that which is most salient.

Wednesday, January 6: Marching right along with Thought, and making improvements to Abstraction. The definition of abstraction given is sufficiently restrictive that they cannot meaningfully be said to have "structure." Therefore, structure must be associated with thought; if the thought is a true thought, then the resulting abstraction would effectively carry the thought's structure.

Monday, January 4: Continuing to work on Abstraction. New definition of true thought in Thought and Belief. In order to make this development mathematically sound, an entity complimentary to abstraction must be introduced (namely, observation).

Thursday, December 31: Rewrote introductory section of Abstraction: Including both definitions of abstraction now. A lot of new material in the works, just having trouble expressing it in a suitably formal manner. Have a happy new year!

Saturday, December 26: Revised thoughts on consciousness in Introduction, and may have arrived at an elegant definition.

Tuesday, December 22: Discarded Processes— I really had hope for them! Rewrote Abstractions to reflect.

Saturday, December 19: Nice little addition to Intellect. While Intellect is the most important component of this research, it is also the last, and, for any given mind, the most variable. In particular, Intellect is true with respect to ego (in the sense that if you have ego, then you must field intellect), but not otherwise, and the theory must reflect that.

Although I still like the definition of abstraction given, it is essential that observation and abstraction belong to the same process; it is therefore proving (more than a little) naive. Moreover, it might even be a mistake to define that process explicitly, given that it may exist only implicitly within the mind.

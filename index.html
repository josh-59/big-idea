Welcome

Hello,

This website presents an attempt to describe, in gory detail, the operation of the mind. Specifically, it seeks to explain, formally, the mind's intellect, under the belief that such an explanation will allow us to replicate it artificially.

Before venturing into the theory itself, it may be worthwhile to affirm the most important and direct observations I have made about the human intellect. These motivate an ongoing pursuit, and promise its eventual conclusion.

The first observation was that my own thoughts are not discrete, but instead follow a particular "semi-discrete" pattern. That is, it is not the case that one has a thought $A$, followed by a thought $B,$ where $A$ and $B$ are unrelated. Rather, Every thought explicitly observes the previous thought.

The second observation was that this could be extended: Sophistication about the mind can be expressed as its ability to observe more than one previous thought.

The above two observations found the entirety of this pursuit, and are to me unquestionable. The only question has been how to express the situation formally.

"Thought" is developed more formallly as abstraction, which, in turn, has been stated in terms of simplification or discarding. More, I have come to conclude that the mind is principally defined by memory.

Except where otherwise noted, all ideas presented here are my own. I've been working on these ideas for some time, and would like to put them into the public domain. Although I've done my best, I wish to consider each essay to be up for review. Please feel free to comment, suggest, or critique.

Enjoy!

– Josh

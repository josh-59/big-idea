Evaluation

This paper gives support for the idea that evaluation, which is for the moment restricted to deductive logic, may be expressed in terms of abstraction.

Example 1: Transitive Law of Implication

The transitive law of implication is a commonly used rule in propositional logic. It states that, given "A implies B", and "B implies C", we may conclude "A implies C."

We are discarding statement B in its entirity.

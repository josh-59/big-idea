Thought

"Our everyday use of language is vague,
and our everyday level of thinking is often muddled."
Dr. Patrick Suppes
Introduction to Logic, page xi

The challenge here is to develop a single model of thought which is capable of accurately describing both vague and precise thinking.

Definition: A thought is a finite sequence of ordered pairs of collections of information $$(a_1, b_1), (a_2,b_2), ..., (a_n, b_n)$$ such that $(a_1, b_n)$ forms an abstraction.

A thought is a pattern of thinking whose specifics ("internals") may be discarded, leaving an abstraction. Note that a thought is not necessarily composed strictly of abstractions.

Intuitively, precise thinking will be associated with abstraction of a single thing. Vague thinking will be associated with "impure" abstraction, or, singular abstractions of more than one thing. This can, in theory, be used to explain both creativity and abductive reasoning. In either case, we do not need to consider sequences of ordered pairs of collections of information that do not form abstractions.

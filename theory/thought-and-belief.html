Thought and Belief

Thought

Definition: A thought is a sequence of ordered pairs of collections of information.

A sequence of abstractions may have the property that it is correct. Intuitively, our aim is to describe "purity" of thought. This is done formally by demanding that the amount of information entertained is monotonically decreasing.

Definition: A true thought is a sequence of abstractions which together form a pure abstraction.

Belief

Definition: A belief is an abstraction present in the argument of an abstraction.

Model

A model is to thought what a mneumonic is to memory.

Definition: A model is an abstraction which supports metathought.

Thought and Belief

"Our everyday use of language is vague,
and our everyday level of thinking is often muddled."
Dr. Patrick Suppes
Introduction to Logic, page xi

Thought

The challenge here is to develop a single model of thought which is capable of accurately describing both vague and precise thinking.

Definition: A thought is a sequence of ordered pairs of collections of information.

Intuitively, vague thinking will be associated with "impure" abstraction, or, singular abstractions of more than one thing. This can, in theory, be used to explain both creativity and abductive reasoning.

Concurrrently, precise thinking will be associated with abstraction of a single thing ("pure" abstraction). This we will express by demanding that the amount of information entertained be decreasing.

Definition: A true thought is a sequence of ordered pairs of collections of information $$(a_1, b_1), (a_2,b_2), ..., (a_n, b_n)$$ such that $(a_1, b_n)$ forms an abstraction.

A true thought is a pattern of thinking whose specifics ("internals") may be discarded, leaving an abstraction. Moreover, a true thought has the property that it may be included in any other thought, and its truth, if any, will be preserved.

Belief

Definition: A belief is an abstraction present in the argument of an abstraction.

Model

A model is to thought what a mneumonic is to memory.

Definition: A model is an abstraction which supports metathought.

Intellect

Definition: An intellect is a indefinite sequence of abstractions.

Intuitively, an intellect is a pattern of thinking; it is not self or ego. In defining the intellect, we ignore the content of thought, and instead concern ourselves with its pattern.

The abstractions that constitute an intellect will be said to be the abstractions of the intellect. We might also say that an intellect fields these abstractions.

The case we will be concerned with is when the abstractions an intellect fields are of constant order, greater than zero. As a consequence, we will refer to intellects by their order:

Conjecture: The human mind fields an intellect with order between one and eight, inclusive.

Depth

The abstractions of an intellect may be constructed as a sequence of multiple, smaller abstractions, which is to say that the abstractions an intellect fields may, themselves, be intellects:

Conjecture: Moreover, the average human intellect has a depth of four.

Intellect As a Data Structure

One approach towards describing the intellect might be as a data structure with particular properties. Specifically, it would be a data structure which is endlessly economizing. Formally, that would be expressed as operating in $O(0)$ (Big-Oh-Zero) time.

Conjecture: Any data structure operating in O(0) time will prove to be an intellect.

This view is as-yet undeveloped, and questions remain: What is its interface? What do I mean by O(0)?

Intellect

Introduction

Pragmatically speaking, Intellect is our goal. Loosely, it is the strategy of simplification chosen by the mind.

For any mind, the strategy of simplification it uses represents a tradeoff: A greater number of concurrent abstractions implies that each must be more extreme (simpler), while a greater number of concurrent abstractions yields greater potential sophistication of the thought process.

Definition: An intellect is a conscious abstracting process; it yields an indefinite sequence of abstractions. We will say that an intellect fields these abstractions.

Intuitively, an intellect is a pattern of thinking. In defining the intellect, we ignore the content of thought, and instead concern ourselves with its patterns.

Ego

Definition: An ego is an intellect's abstraction of itself.

Introspection

Definition: Introspection is the tendency of the intellect to simplify itself.

Introspection begins an inquiry into intelligence; it is related to pattern-finding and statement-making.

Intellect As a Data Structure

One approach towards describing the intellect might be as a data structure with the property that it is endlessly economizing. Formally, this would be expressed as $O(0)$ (Big-Oh-Zero) time.

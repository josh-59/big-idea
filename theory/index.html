Theory

"All non-trivial abstractions, to some degree, are leaky."
Joel Spolsky
The Law of Leaky Abstractions

Introduction— We begin our investigation by defining the mind to be part of the body.

Processes— This paper introduces the process, a function without fixed points. Processes will form the mathematical backbone of this theory.

Observation and Abstraction— Observation is the act of accruing new information; abstraction is the act of discarding it.

Thought and Belief— A thought is a sequence of abstractions.

Intellect— Pragmatically speaking, Intellect is our goal. Loosely, it is the strategy of simplification chosen by the mind.

Tracing— A process could, potentially, "trace" an object; in leau of proper formal definitions, the concept is presented intuitively, via animation.

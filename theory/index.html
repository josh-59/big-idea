"All non-trivial abstractions, to some degree, are leaky."
Joel Spolsky
The Law of Leaky Abstractions

Introduction— We begin our investigation of the mind with the assumption that the mind is a component of the human body, where the body is separate from the world.

Processes— This paper introduces the process, a function without fixed points. Processes will form the mathematical backbone of this theory.

Abstraction— An abstraction discards information; intuitively, an abstraction is a simplification. Every abstraction acts like a mathematical function, with distinct input and output. Because abstractions exist in multiple contexts, we must differentiate them in a meaningful way.

Intellect— An intellect is sequence of abstractions of finite order.

Tracing— A process could, potentially, "trace" an object; in leau of proper formal definitions, the concept is presented intuitively, via animation.

Theory

Introduction— We begin our investigation by defining the mind to be part of the body.

Abstraction— Observation is the act of accruing new information; abstraction is the act of discarding it.

Thought— Thought is a sequence of observations and abstractions which forms an abstraction.

Intellect— Pragmatically speaking, Intellect is our goal. Loosely, it is the strategy of simplification chosen by the mind.

Tracing— A process could, potentially, "trace" an object; in leau of proper formal definitions, the concept is presented intuitively, via animation.

Thread

Defintion: A thread is a sequence of observations and abstractions acting upon a container of information in which the container starts empty, ends empty, and which includes a thought.

In order to guarantee that the thread contains a thought, it is only necessary to assert that:

  1. On the upward (first) portion, the container of information is conscious of its surroundings (that is, accepting input)
  2. On the downward (second) portion, the container of information is conscious of itself

For each thread, there exists an underlying process which determines the operations of the thread. We will say that this process traces the thread.

Abstraction

"Our everyday use of language is vague,
and our everyday level of thinking is often muddled."
Dr. Patrick Suppes
Introduction to Logic, page xi

In my opinion, abstractions are fundamental to the mind: Through abstraction, the objects of the mind and the activity of thinking may be related in a fundamental way.

Definition: An abstraction is a mapping from some information to less information.

Since an abstraction is an ordered pair, it is a collection of information. Moreover, it contains two distinct collections of information.

Mathematically, we can either observe an abstraction, and obtain an ordered pair; or, we can apply an abstraction. In either case, we obtain a collection of information.

Computationally, each abstraction may be represented as a filter. Intuitively, the purpose of entertaining abstractions is to filter information.

Abstraction

"Our everyday use of language is vague,
and our everyday level of thinking is often muddled."
Dr. Patrick Suppes
Introduction to Logic, page xi

Abstraction is the act of discarding information. Intuitively, abstraction is simplification.

Definition: An abstraction is any realization of a discarding process.

Because it is our goal to describe the mind, we must describe the "muddled" thinking mentioned above.

Definition: A pure abstraction is an abstraction of exactly one thing. An impure abstraction is an abstraction of at least two things.

Introduction

We begin our investigation by defining the mind to be part of the body:

Since we are restricting our attention to the mind's intellect, we must only consider two components of the mind: Memory, and Consciousness. Within this theory, observation by the mind is writing to the mind's memory. The mind's consciousness is the process responsible for this.

Axioms of the Mind's Memory

1) The mind is constantly forgetting.

This statement could be realized indirectly by continual over-writing of a fixed quantity of memory.

2) The essence of pain is that it is difficult to forget.

The pain associated with any particular memory will be a single-dimensional quantity, aversion, which describes fear-of the associated memory. As yet, this is the only datapoint associated with each memory.

One reason to frame this discussion in the negative (via pain, rather than love, say) is that doing so mitigates the possibility that the mind would be drawn to itself.

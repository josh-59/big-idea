Introduction

We begin by defining the mind is the part of the body that accepts input and yields output:

Our principle object of development is the intellect: That part of the mind which carries out explicit thinking. This definition will allow us to place bounds on our development of the intellect. In particular, long-term memory will be viewed as a feature of the mind, operating in tandem with the mind's intellect. Further, emotions will emerge implicitly, as a consequence of the intellect's thoughts.

We will be severely restricting the faculties which are available to us by considering only memory, and, the activity of writing to it, here referred to as consciousness.

Necessarily, we must define thought; this is the subject of the next chapter.

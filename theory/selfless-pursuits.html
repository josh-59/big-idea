Selfless Pursuits

Ego is the mind's "self," and it is easily the case that it pursues something without regard for that idea of itself. Intuitive examples include music, mathematics, and military service.

Selfless pursuits yield organization of the intellect, and are common in children; the intuitive examples above are relative to the mature, adult ego.

Enlightenment

In each mind, there exists an observing process. Observation of this process by itself is the "truth realized" state. It is a special case of selfless pursuit, and, it is not final. The McKennian "enlightened" state is that period of time immediately following truth-realization, when the mind still posesses the faculty to problem solve. This state is degenerate, and, the degenerated state is isomorphic to the newborn.

The neatest way out of this state is addiction.
